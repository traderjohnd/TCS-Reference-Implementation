# Clinical Decision Support Protocols (2026)

## Scope

This document covers general clinical decision support protocols used in
adult and pediatric inpatient settings. It is the working policy for AI
systems providing clinical decision support, consistent with ISO 13485
quality management, ISO 14971 risk management, and IEC 62304 software
lifecycle standards.

## Recommendation Authority

AI-generated clinical recommendations are **advisory only**. Final
clinical decisions rest with the responsible licensed clinician. Every
recommendation must:

1. Be traceable to a documented clinical source (peer-reviewed
   guideline, hospital protocol, FDA-approved label, or this protocol
   document).
2. Disclose the source version and publication date.
3. Flag any out-of-distribution case (rare presentation, off-label
   indication, novel combination) for physician review.

## Standard Workflow

1. **Identity verification.** Confirm the patient identity through
   the EHR record. AI systems must not provide patient-specific
   recommendations without a verified patient context.
2. **Allergy and current medication review.** Cross-check the patient's
   active medication list against the recommendation for known
   interactions (see drug_interactions.md).
3. **Indication confirmation.** Verify the recommendation falls within
   labeled or evidence-supported indications. Off-label recommendations
   require explicit clinician acknowledgment.
4. **Risk-benefit documentation.** Record the clinical reasoning
   linking patient state to recommendation in the EHR.

## Documentation Requirements

Every recommendation generated by an AI clinical decision support
system must include:

- Patient ID (verified)
- Source guideline reference with version
- Recommendation summary
- Risk-benefit notes
- Clinician acknowledgment

## Off-Label and Novel Combinations

Off-label use and novel drug combinations carry elevated risk and
require:

- Documented clinical rationale
- Risk assessment per ISO 14971
- Senior clinician sign-off
- Post-administration monitoring plan

Recommendations involving off-label pediatric dosing of cardiac
medications, novel oncologic combinations, or experimental therapies
must be escalated for human review.
