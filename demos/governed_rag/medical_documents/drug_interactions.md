# Drug Interaction Reference (2026)

## Prohibited Combinations (Hard Stop)

The following drug combinations are **prohibited** under the clinical
decision support policy and must never appear in any AI-generated
recommendation. These are absolute contraindications based on
well-documented adverse interactions and FDA safety communications.

### Warfarin + macrolide antibiotics

**Prohibited combinations:**

- Warfarin + clarithromycin
- Warfarin + erythromycin
- Warfarin + azithromycin (at therapeutic anticoagulation doses)

**Rationale:** Macrolide antibiotics inhibit CYP3A4 and CYP1A2,
significantly increasing warfarin serum concentration and INR.
Combination dramatically elevates the risk of major bleeding,
particularly in patients with prior GI bleed history.

**Required action:** Recommend an alternative antibiotic. If macrolide
therapy is clinically necessary, anticoagulation must be reassessed
by hematology before macrolide initiation.

### Methotrexate + trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole

Increased methotrexate toxicity through reduced renal clearance.
Prohibited in standard outpatient prescribing.

### MAOIs + serotonergic agents

Hypertensive crisis and serotonin syndrome risk. Includes SSRIs, SNRIs,
tramadol, meperidine, dextromethorphan, and certain triptans.

### Linezolid + serotonergic agents

Serotonin syndrome risk via MAOI-like mechanism. Co-administration
prohibited; washout period required.

## High-Risk Combinations (Compliance Review Required)

The following combinations require explicit clinical reasoning and
compliance officer review:

1. **NSAIDs + anticoagulants** — bleeding risk; alternative analgesia
   preferred for patients on warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban, or dabigatran.
2. **ACE inhibitors + potassium-sparing diuretics** — hyperkalemia risk;
   requires baseline and follow-up potassium monitoring.
3. **Statins + strong CYP3A4 inhibitors** — rhabdomyolysis risk; dose
   reduction or alternative statin required.

## Pediatric-Specific Cautions

Pediatric prescribing requires weight-based dosing verification and
explicit confirmation that the recommended dose is age-appropriate. Many
adult prescribing assumptions do not apply.

For neonatal and infant prescribing, consult the latest *Neonatal
Pharmacology Handbook* and the institution's pediatric pharmacy team.
