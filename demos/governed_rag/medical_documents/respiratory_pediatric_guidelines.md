# Pediatric Respiratory Care Guidelines (2026)

## Scope

Covers pediatric pneumonia, respiratory failure, and sepsis management
for ages 0 to 17 in inpatient and emergency settings.

## Pediatric Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP)

### Initial Assessment

1. **Respiratory rate, oxygen saturation, work of breathing.**
2. **Chest X-ray** for moderate-to-severe presentations.
3. **CBC, CRP, blood culture** if febrile or septic appearance.
4. **Respiratory viral panel** during seasonal peaks.

### First-line Empiric Therapy

**For ages 3 months to 5 years (typical bacterial CAP):**
- Outpatient: amoxicillin 90 mg/kg/day divided BID
- Inpatient: ampicillin IV 200 mg/kg/day divided q6h

**For ages 5 to 17 (atypical pathogens covered):**
- Outpatient: amoxicillin + azithromycin
- Inpatient: ampicillin + azithromycin IV

**Penicillin-allergic:**
- Clindamycin or third-generation cephalosporin per institutional protocol.

## Pediatric Sepsis Bundle

Within the first hour of recognized sepsis:

1. **Vascular access and labs** (cultures before antibiotics if feasible)
2. **Crystalloid bolus** 20 mL/kg, reassess perfusion
3. **Empiric broad-spectrum antibiotics** within 60 minutes
4. **Vasopressors** if fluid-refractory shock

## Pediatric Respiratory Failure

Indications for escalation to PICU:

- Persistent hypoxemia despite supplemental oxygen
- Increasing work of breathing
- Mental status changes
- Hemodynamic instability

Mechanical ventilation strategies for pediatric ARDS follow the
PALICC-2 guidelines (Pediatric Acute Lung Injury Consensus Conference).

## Documentation Requirements

Every pediatric respiratory case must document:

- Weight, age, and PEWS (Pediatric Early Warning Score) at presentation
- Antimicrobial choice rationale with allergy review
- Response to initial therapy at 24 and 48 hours
- Senior clinician sign-off for ICU transfers

## When to Hold or Escalate AI Recommendations

AI-generated pediatric recommendations must be **held for human review**
when:

- Patient weight or age is missing from the patient context
- Recommended dose falls outside published pediatric ranges
- Recommendation involves off-label medication use
- The presentation includes high novelty features (rare combination
  of comorbidities, atypical pathogens, age-extreme presentations)

These are governance, not clinical, holds: the recommendation may
ultimately be correct, but human verification is required before
delivery.
